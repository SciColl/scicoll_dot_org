Organization

Secretariat Office

The SciColl Secretariat Office operated from November 2013 through January 2017, housed at the Smithsonian Institution, Washington, D.C., USA.

The Secretariat Office provided the following core services:

  • Organization of Executive Board meetings and other periodic reporting to members and Executive Board.
  • Establishment of website and online network to facilitate communication among members and their communities.
  • Development of outreach material and a campaign about scientific collections, their impacts and our activities.
  • Development of a Program of Work and budget, to be approved by the Executive Board.
  • Soliciation for additional funding for priority activities that were not supported by the general budget.

Eileen Graham, Program Manager

Eileen was SciColl's program manager for the duration of the project. She was responsible for the day-to-day operations of SciColl, working directly with the chair of the Executive Board to ensure that SciColl priorities established by the Board were implemented. Eileen also served on the U.S. Interagency Working Group for Scientific Collections (IWGSC), which was responsible for similar initiatives to SciColl at the U.S. Federal level.

Prior to joining SciColl, Eileen worked as a consultant to the US National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration. In that role she was in charge of managing a large collection of samples associated with the Deepwater Horizon oil spill and damage assessment. That position required both a cross-disciplinary approach to processing and data acquisition, and the maintenance of samples held under litigation-appropriate chain-of-custody procedures.

Eileen is a coastal and marine ecologist with experience working on all three coasts of the United States.

Executive Board

Representatives from members organizations made up the Executive Board, which set priorities that were implemented by the Secretariat Office.
