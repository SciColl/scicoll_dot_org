Member Organizations

We are a membership-based organization that officially launched in April 2013 with 10 founding members: three nations and seven institutions. Representatives of members make up an Executive Board. The Executive Board sets priorities that are implemented by the Secretariat Office.

Member Benefits

Our members participate in shaping the direction of our program through representation on the Executive Board, which develops the direction and priorities of our program of work.

Additionally, the benefits of membership include demonstrating leadership in new research and uses of collections, first-line access to diverse collections and networks, visibility of hosting conferences, providing input into our outreach activities and involvement in citizen science innovations.

Members join our program by signing a Memorandum of Understanding, which includes a three-year financial commitment. The annual contributions made by member countries and institutions depend on the Gross Expenditure for Research and Development in their country or the size of their institution as measured by annual operating budget and staff size.
